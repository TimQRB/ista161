Introduction

The ISTA 161 course is coming to an end and I decided to summarize everything we studied on one website. I am very satisfied with this course even though we did not touch on programming we studied topics related to ethics and data which I also think is important.

Week 1

Basics of Ethics and a Digital World

This week was focused on how digital technologies are changing our world and why ethics are so important in the digital age. We figured out what digitization (the transformation of information into digital form) is and how it differs from digitalization (the more profound changes in society and culture that digital technologies bring).

We also discussed important ethical issues, for instance, how to maintain privacy, protect data, ensure security, fairness, transparency, accountability. Governments is trying to solve these problems with laws and regulations for AI so that ethics are not violated but I think that today's laws are not suitable for the modern world as they were written only at the beginning of new technologies so reforms are needed. For my part, I have tried to use visual images to show and explain with examples why ethics are important and why companies should not forget about ethics when working with their users.

Week 2

Data Life Cycle and Acquisition

During this week, I realized that data management is not only about analyzing people's data but also about responsibility. Data goes through a complete life cycle from creation and storage to analysis and final utilization. Each stage requires careful organization and an ethical approach. I also described from my own experience that most data is unstructured and to fix this the data must be processed each time. Every company needs high-quality data and working with it is a time-consuming process. I also said that ethics and data are linked because when working with people's data it is necessary to take into account the process of confidentiality and privacy. This is essential because every business wants to have a good reputation.

Eight stages of data lifecycle management

Week 3

Data Cleaning

The main lesson I learned from this part of the course is that data quality is more important than algorithms or code. Even the most advanced algorithm is worthless if it is based on inaccurate, incomplete or poorly structured data. Therefore, data quality management is the cornerstone of research data management and careful data processing is essential at all stages. I also shared my experience of how exhausting and routine the data preparation process can be. Analysis, cleaning, processing and validation are all difficult and time-consuming tasks but with their support you can build a good project in which all the statistics will be visible and demonstrated for future plans. Ethics is also a cornerstone that must be taken into account when working with data, whatever it may be. Data leaks can lead not only to reputational damage but also to legal proceedings which can mean the end for any business.

Week 4

Data Use and Reuse

This part of the course described where and how data is stored because this is a fundamental part of ethical data management. It is impossible to protect information if you do not understand its structure, location, where the database dump or project repository is located. I gave an example of working on a project where a lot of student data was stored and working with it was challenging. There were many restrictions, requirements and cases that needed to be taken into account. The project optimized university admissions but it was necessary to take absolutely all documents into consider. After processing all the data, it had to be used for analysis and, most importantly, carefully stored. Proper management, transparency, and awareness of limitations are necessary for building reliable systems. Real data reflects the true state of the project and based on this, new modules and features can be added to the project.

Data use and reuse in the lifecycle

Plan → Collect → Assure → Describe → Archive → Share → Reuse → Discover → Integrate → Analyze → Plan

Week 5

Publish Data

After completing this stage of the course, I gained a more comprehensive understanding of academic integrity particularly with regard to self-plagiarism. My initial belief was that I could freely use my own writings without any limitations. However, I discovered that in the academic and scientific world reusing one's own previous work without proper attribution can still be considered unethical. I also showed the functionality of an article verification system that detects plagiarism by checking works not only for similarities with other students' materials but also with previously submitted works. So, in the 21st century, I think it is much more difficult to appropriate someone else's content but nevertheless, transparency and honesty are fundamental principles of academic culture.

Week 6

Data Preserve and Destroy

During this section of the course, I learned about the role of data storage and preservation. Data management goes beyond simple collection and analysis as it includes ensuring data availability and security over a long period of time. Effective archiving allows companies and organizations to reuse data in the future. I am a fan of old video games and movies but I cannot legally download or buy them in stores because their repositories have been lost or their licenses have expired. Modern laws for digital distribution were written in the 1980s and 1990s and have not changed globally since then which I believe needs to be changed because today everything is completely different. I understand that recovery can be laborious, costly or even impossible which is especially critical in science and business. So the ethics here are much deeper and that is why I support any efforts to reform laws relating to digital distribution and storage.
